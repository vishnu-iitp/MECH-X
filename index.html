üõ†Ô∏è Hardware Platform Guide: Detailed Comparison & Selection

Arduino vs Raspberry Pi vs ESP32 vs Jetson Boards

Each platform serves different purposes: Arduino for real-time control, Raspberry Pi for IoT and AI, ESP32 for wireless connectivity, and Jetson for advanced AI/robotics applications.

üü¢

BEGINNER LEVEL (‚Çπ8,000-12,000)

Arduino Uno R3 (‚Çπ2,000)

Perfect for learning basics - 16MHz, 32KB flash, 14 digital pins. Huge community support with 100,000+ tutorials.

Best for: LED control, sensor reading, servo motors, simple robotics
Pros: Beginner-friendly, extensive libraries, plug-and-play
Raspberry Pi 4 (‚Çπ6,000)

Quad-core ARM CPU, 4GB RAM, full Linux OS. Mini computer perfect for IoT and computer vision.

Best for: Home automation, web servers, AI projects, media centers
Pros: Full OS, WiFi/Bluetooth built-in, GPIO pins, camera support
Essential Components:
  • Breadboard + jumper wires
  • LEDs, resistors, sensors
  • Servo motors (SG90)
  • LCD display 16x2
  • Temperature & ultrasonic sensors
üü°

INTERMEDIATE LEVEL (‚Çπ16,000-24,000)

ESP32 DevKit (‚Çπ1,200)

240MHz dual-core, 520KB RAM, WiFi/Bluetooth. Low power consumption, perfect for IoT.

Best for: Smart home devices, wireless sensors, mesh networks, BLE projects
Pros: Built-in WiFi/BT, low power, multiple protocols, Arduino compatible
Jetson Nano (‚Çπ12,000)

Quad-core ARM A57, 128-core Maxwell GPU, 4GB RAM. CUDA support for AI/ML acceleration.

Best for: Computer vision, object detection, autonomous robots, edge AI
Pros: GPU acceleration, TensorRT, ROS support, multiple camera inputs
Additional Components:
  • Camera modules (OV5640, IMX219)
  • Stepper motors + drivers
  • Level shifters & voltage regulators
  • Wireless modules (LoRa, Zigbee)
  • 3D printer filament (PLA/PETG)
